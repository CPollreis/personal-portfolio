Working on the FSAE accumulator left me buried in battery-management logs — cell
voltages, temperatures, fault codes, thousands of CAN frames per run. The **BMS
Local Agent** is my attempt to make that data conversational without sending any
of it to the cloud.

## What it does

Point it at a log (or a live CAN feed) and ask questions in plain English:

- *"Which cell had the widest voltage swing during the last run?"*
- *"Did any thermistor cross the warning threshold, and when?"*
- *"Summarize the faults in this session."*

The agent parses the telemetry into a local **SQLite** store, and a locally-run
LLM (via **Ollama**) translates questions into queries over that structured
data, then explains the results in context.

## Why local

Battery data is sensitive and I wanted something that works at the track with no
connectivity. Everything — parsing, storage, and inference — runs on the laptop.
The LLM never sees raw frames directly; it works against a compact schema, which
keeps answers grounded in the actual measurements instead of hallucinated
numbers.

It's become the fastest way for me to triage a run: instead of scrubbing plots,
I just ask.

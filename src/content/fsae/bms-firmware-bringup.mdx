The accumulator is the most safety-critical system on an electric FSAE car, and
the BMS firmware is what stands between a healthy pack and a very bad day. This
entry walks through bring-up on our segment monitoring boards.

## Talking to the cells

Each segment uses a monitoring IC that we reach over **isoSPI** for isolation.
The first milestone was just reading all cell voltages and thermistors reliably:

- Wrote the low-level driver as a small state machine: wake, configure, start
  ADC, read, verify PEC.
- Every packet is checked against its packet-error code; a bad PEC retries, and
  repeated failures escalate to a fault.

## Faults are the feature

Cell monitoring is the easy 80%. The firmware earns its keep in how it handles
the edges: over/under-voltage, over-temperature, an open sense wire, or a stale
reading. Each maps to a defined response, and the pack contactors open on any
unrecoverable fault. We built a small fault table so the logic is auditable
rather than scattered through the code.

## Reporting out

Finally, the BMS publishes state of charge, min/max cell voltage, and
temperatures onto the vehicle **CAN bus** so the dash, data logger, and (this
season) the autonomous system all share the same picture of pack health.

Getting a clean, isolated read of every cell, and trusting it enough to gate
the contactors, was one of the most satisfying bring-ups I've done.
